Raumlaissi jaarituksi

An interactive translation of Frans Hjalmar Nortamo's 1898 short story “Antonim bäev”

April 15, 2025
PDF Report ↗

Having been established in 1442, Rauma is the fifth oldest city in Finland. It is located on the west coast in the Satakunta province. Since its inception, Rauma has been a major port and trade hub. This relationship with greater Europe has brought not just goods and cargo to Finland’s shores but also linguistic influence. The Rauma dialect is known to be one of the strongest dialects of Finnish with influences from Swedish, English, Estonian, French, Russian, and German due to the town’s seafaring culture. The dialect is so strong that many prefer to refer to it as the Rauma language. The stronger, historical form of Rauma language is used to this day in city signage and is spoken by a small population in the area. The language is maintained and revitalized by Nortamo Seor (“The Nortamo Society”), named after the prominent Rauma author Frans Hjalmar Nortamo.

Nortamo wrote songs and several collections of short stories in the Rauma dialect. The first volume Raumlaissi jaarituksi was published in 1898 in Rauma’s yacht club journal and contained a series of humorous tall tales about the lives of Rauma residents. Aside from being a pivotal work of Rauma literature and culture, it is also the very first piece of dialectal Finnish literature whatsoever. Publishing in dialects was strictly banned from the standardization of the Finnish language until Nortamo’s writings. None of Nortamo’s writings have been rendered in English, and they are in the public domain.

The purpose of this project is to translate “Antonim bäev” (“Anton’s Name Day”), the first short story in the collection, into English for the very first time so that it may be enjoyed by a wider audience.
